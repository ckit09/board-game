# Game Rules & Guide

## Chess Rules

### Piece Movement

**Pawn (♟/♙):**
- Moves forward 1 square (2 squares on first move)
- Captures diagonally forward

**Knight (♞/♘):**
- Moves in L-shape (2 squares in one direction, 1 square perpendicular)
- Only piece that can jump over others

**Bishop (♝/♗):**
- Moves diagonally any number of squares
- Stays on same color squares

**Rook (♜/♖):**
- Moves horizontally or vertically any number of squares
- Powerful piece in endgame

**Queen (♛/♕):**
- Moves any direction (horizontal, vertical, diagonal) any number of squares
- Strongest piece

**King (♚/♔):**
- Moves one square in any direction
- Most important piece - if mated, you lose

### Game Rules

- White always goes first
- You must move if you have valid moves
- Game ends when:
  - Player cannot move (checkmate or stalemate)
  - One side resigns
  - All pieces of one color are captured

### Playing Tips

1. Protect your king early
2. Develop pieces toward the center
3. Control key squares
4. Look for tactical opportunities
5. In AI mode, undo moves to learn from mistakes

## Gomoku Rules

### Basic Rules

1. **Players:** Two players (black and white)
2. **Board:** 15×15 grid
3. **Turn:** Black plays first, then players alternate
4. **Move:** Place one stone on empty intersection per turn
5. **Win:** First player to get 5 in a row wins

### Five in a Row

Stones can be arranged:
- Horizontally: ⬤⬤⬤⬤⬤
- Vertically: (stacked)
- Diagonally: ⬤ ⬤ ⬤ ⬤ ⬤ (any direction)

### Game States

- **Active:** Game in progress
- **Win:** One player has 5 in a row
- **Draw:** Board fills with no winner (rare in Gomoku)

### Playing Tips

**Opening:**
- Start near the center
- Don't waste moves on edge pieces
- Control the middle 5×5 area

**Mid-Game:**
- Create threats (4 in a row)
- Block opponent's threats
- Look for setup positions

**Endgame:**
- One threat usually wins
- Two threats = victory
- Block carefully to avoid traps

**AI Strategy Notes:**
- AI prioritizes squares near existing stones
- It will secure a win if possible
- It will block your winning moves
- Gets stronger as board fills

## General Strategy

### Chess Strategy

**Opening (First 10-15 moves):**
- Control center squares
- Develop pieces toward center
- Castle early for king safety
- Don't move same piece twice

**Middle Game (Mid-game tactics):**
- Look for piece attacks
- Create pins and forks
- Maintain piece safety
- Control key squares

**Endgame (Few pieces left):**
- King becomes strong
- Pawns become important, can promote
- Passed pawns are valuable
- Trade pieces if ahead

### Gomoku Strategy

**Opening:**
- Tengen (center) is strongest
- Play near the center star points
- Create base for expansion

**Territory Control:**
- 5×5 center is crucial
- Two threats = automatic win
- Prevent opponent from creating threats

**Attack & Defense:**
- Block at 5 when possible
- Create multiple threats
- Use AI's responses to predict next move

## Controls

### Chess
- **Click piece** → Select (shows valid moves in green)
- **Click valid square** → Move piece
- **New Game** → Start fresh game
- **Undo** → Take back last move(s)

### Gomoku
- **Click empty square** → Place stone
- **New Game** → Start fresh game
- **Undo** → Take back last move(s)
- **Mode selector** → Switch between AI and Player-vs-Player

## FAQ

**Q: Can I undo multiple times in AI mode?**
A: Yes! When playing against AI, undo removes both your move and the AI's move.

**Q: How hard is the AI?**
A: The AI is beginner-friendly:
- Chess: Makes random valid moves (no strategic planning)
- Gomoku: Tries to win and blocks your wins

**Q: Can I play with a friend?**
A: Yes! Select "Player vs Player" mode in either game.

**Q: What if I make a mistake?**
A: Use the Undo button (works on most moves).

**Q: Is there a timer?**
A: No, play at your own pace.
